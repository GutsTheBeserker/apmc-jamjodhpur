Knowledge Base

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to help you navigate APMC guidelines, schedules, bidding protocols, and support programs.

What documents do farmers need to sell crops at APMC Jamjodhpur?

Farmers need to carry their Aadhaar card, 7/12 Land Registry documents (ખેતી પત્રક), bank passbook copy for instant e-transfer, and the Digital Gate Pass generated online or at the entrance kiosk.

How does the e-NAM bidding system work?

Once the crop arrives, a sample is graded for quality parameters. The grading details are uploaded to the national e-NAM portal. Verified traders from all over India view these details and submit bids electronically. The farmer has the right to accept or reject the highest electronic bid.

Are there storage facilities available for crops?

Yes, we have high-capacity warehouses and cold storage units. Farmers can secure their crops at extremely nominal subsidized rates to avoid distressed sales during price drops, allowing them to sell when rates improve.

How can inter-state traders participate in auctions?

Inter-state traders must register on the unified e-NAM portal, possess a valid GST registration, and submit a security deposit to get trading clearances for Jamjodhpur APMC auctions.
